Good Morning

Monday, June 1, 2015

NSA phone collection authority expires, Biden’s son dies and more in today’s Briefing.

No Collect Calls

As of midnight last night, the National Security Agency (NSA) is no longer collecting bulk data on phone calls. Republican presidential hopeful and Senator Rand Paul delivered on his promise to force the Patriot Act to expire yesterday.

But Paul’s efforts aren’t likely to kill the NSA’s spying capabilities permanently. The Senate is expected to pass the USA Freedom Act, which proponents say would reform – but not end – the NSA’s powers. The bill overwhelmingly passed the House two weeks ago.

The debate over the NSA’s surveillance methods boils down to the balance between privacy and security. Libertarians like Paul, as well as many Democrats, favor privacy; the Republican establishment prioritizes security.

Joe Biden Loses Son

Beau Biden, the 46-year-old son of Vice President Joe Biden, died of brain cancer on Saturday. Biden was an Iraq War veteran and served two terms as Delaware’s attorney general. He was planning to run for governor in 2016.

This is the second major tragedy to visit the vice president’s family. In 1972, Biden’s first wife and their 1-year-old daughter were killed in a car accident. Beau and his younger brother barely survived the crash.

Rough Road

Ross Ulbricht, the founder of the black market website Silk Road, was sentenced to life in prison without the possibility of parole on Friday.

Silk Road facilitated the sale of hundreds of millions of dollars’ worth of illegal drugs. Ulbricht, who’s 31, is also facing separate charges for allegedly attempting to hire assassins. Wired has an excellent two-part series on the “rise and fall” of Silk Road.

GOP Field Grows

South Carolina Senator Lindsey Graham will officially launch his bid for president today. Alongside Senator John McCain, Graham is regarded as one of the Republican Party’s key voices for a strong, and critics would say, aggressive, U.S. foreign policy.

There are now nine official GOP candidates. Former New York governor George Pataki entered the race late last week.
